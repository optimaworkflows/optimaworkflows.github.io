Here's Exactly What We're Asking For — And What We're Not

To run appointment booking through your own phone number, we need a small, specific piece of Twilio access. This page explains exactly what that is and how to hand it over safely — in about five minutes, without giving us your password or your Twilio account itself.

Access Scope · Twilio ISSUED TO: OPTIMA WORKFLOWS

What we access

  • ✓ Send & receive SMS on the one number you assign
  • ✓ Place & log calls tied to that same number
  • ✓ Read delivery/response status for messages we send

What we never touch

  • ✕ Your Twilio password or account login
  • ✕ Billing, invoices, or payment methods
  • ✕ Any other number on your account
  • ✕ Sub-accounts, users, or account settings
Access is granted through a scoped API key you create — not your master credentials. Revocable by you at any time, in one click, from your own Twilio console.

Why We Need This at All

This service books appointments by texting and calling your old, uncontacted leads — and it has to happen from your agency's own number, not ours. We're not asking for this out of convenience; there isn't a version of this service that works without it.

Why your number, not ours

A shared or third-party number gets flagged by carrier spam filters fast. Your registered number carries your agency's existing sender reputation, which is what keeps messages landing in an inbox instead of a spam folder.

Why a key, not your login

A Twilio API key does one job: it lets an app send and receive on your behalf. It can't touch billing or your account settings, and you can delete it the moment you want us gone — no password change required on your end.

How to Create Your Key — Three Steps

You do this from your own Twilio console. We never see your password at any point in this process.

Open your Twilio Console

Log in at your own Twilio account, then go to Account → API keys & tokens.

console.twilio.com → API keys & tokens ↗

Create a new Standard API key

Click Create API key, name it something you'll recognize later — e.g. Optima Workflows — Appointments — and choose key type Standard. Twilio will show you an SID and a Secret.

Copy both values into the form below

Twilio only shows the Secret once — copy it immediately. If you lose it, just delete that key and create a new one; it costs nothing and takes a minute.
